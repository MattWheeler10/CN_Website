1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

When creating a website one of the biggest things to be concerned about i the copyright act! Copyright denies you access to other people's words, images, videos and even colours. The act allows authors and artists the exclusive rights to reproduce, distribute and display their works. The easiest way to avoid copyright is to simply use original content only and only use things of of the internet if they are clearly non copyright. Content without the copyright symbol are still protected by the act so do ensure your not using anything that specifically does not say non copyright. Another constraint is the data protection act which has been spoken about in unit 1, outcome 1. Another law that needs to be followed is the computer misuse act, this defines and criminalises a number of offences involving the use of a computer. This is why it is very serious if an individual is hacking or similar offences as it is criminal and it can result in you getting into serious trouble.

1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome them

When making a website you want it to be accessible by almost everyone and so it is important to include features that make it easier whether it is as simple as having subtitles on a video or more complex, for example having a narrator so that blind people or visually impared users can use your website as well. A colour blind mode could also be included to help people who are colour blind. By making your website simple and easy to read and use is also an effective way of helping people with mental issues confusing fonts and colours can make it hard for these individuals to understand so making a website simplistic is often important to include anyone who wants to use your website.
